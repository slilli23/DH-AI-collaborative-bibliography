# DH-AI Collaborative Bibliography

The Italian Observatory on AI and Digital Humanities (DH-AI) is a project launched
in late 2025 as a Special Interest Group (SIG) of the Italian Association for Digital
Humanities and Digital Culture (AIUCD). Among the SIG's core activities is the creation
of an open collaborative bibliography on AI and DH topics.

This repository contains supplementary material for the paper presented at the
EADH 2026 Conference.

## Contents

1. `bibliography_export_2026-06-30.csv` ‚ÄĒ DH-AI Zotero bibliography (249 items).
2. `tagging_guidelines.pdf` ‚ÄĒ instructions issued to contributors for the
   collaborative tagging task (language: italian).
3. `item_types.html` ‚ÄĒ distribution of items by type (interactive chart).
4. `language_attribution.csv` ‚ÄĒ language assigned to each item, joinable to the
   export on `Key`. Taken from the Zotero `Language` field where compiled (173
   items) and inferred from title and abstract with `langdetect` where missing
   (76 items); the `source` column records which.
5. `tag_mapping.csv` ‚ÄĒ tag normalisation table. The draft was generated by an LLM
   (Claude Opus 4.8) and subsequently validated by hand. Each of the 391 original
   tags is assigned an action (keep / merge / facet / drop) and, where applicable,
   a canonical concept. A *canonical concept* is the single reference form to which several original tags
denoting the same notion are reduced (`AI`, `Artifical intelligence` ‚Üí `Artificial
intelligence`; `etica`, `ethics` ‚Üí `Ethics`). Acronyms are expanded, non-English tags
are rendered in English, and typographical errors are corrected; the resulting form is
recorded in `canonical_concept`, while `graph_label` holds the abbreviation used to
label the network nodes. Four tags (`ricerca`, `scuola`, `universit√†`, `tutti`) mark the intended audience of an
item rather than its subject matter, and are marked `facet` in the mapping: they are
excluded from the co-occurrence network, where they would link unrelated concepts by
construction.
6. `network_edgelist.csv`, `network_metrics.csv`, `network.html` ‚ÄĒ co-occurrence
   network of the normalised concepts.

## Reproducibility

Scripts: `analysis.py`, `normalize_tags.py`, `network_analysis.py`.
Concept co-occurrence is projected from the bipartite item‚Äďconcept graph with Newman
weighting (an item indexing *n* concepts contributes 1/(*n*‚ąí1) to each pair). Concepts
occurring in fewer than 2 items are excluded. Communities are detected with the Louvain
algorithm (seed 42). Python 3.12; pandas, plotly, langdetect, networkx, pyvis.
